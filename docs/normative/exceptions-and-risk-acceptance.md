# Exceptions and risk acceptance

Exceptions are controlled decisions, not informal permissions to ignore requirements.

## Non-waivable requirements

A profile or assessment scheme must identify requirements that cannot be waived because departure would invalidate the framework claim, undermine legal duties, create unacceptable safety or rights risk, or make the conformance statement misleading.

Core `MUST NOT` requirements are presumed non-waivable unless the requirement entry expressly permits an exception class.

## Exception record

Every approved exception must identify:

- requirement identifier;
- affected object and scope;
- requesting and approving authorities;
- reason the requirement cannot currently be met;
- risk analysis, including affected parties;
- compensating controls;
- residual risk owner;
- start and expiry dates;
- monitoring conditions;
- remediation plan;
- revocation trigger;
- public-disclosure status.

## Decision authority

The body responsible for implementing a requirement must not be the sole approver of its own material exception. High-impact exceptions require independent assurance or oversight review.

## Time limitation

Exceptions must be time-bounded. Renewal is a new decision supported by current evidence. Repeated renewal should trigger a requirement, profile or operating-model review rather than normalising permanent nonconformity.

## Conformance effect

A conformance claim must disclose exceptions material to its interpretation. An exception cannot preserve a claim where the assessment scheme defines the affected requirement as a blocking requirement.
