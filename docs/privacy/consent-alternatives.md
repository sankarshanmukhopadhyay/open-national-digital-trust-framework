# Consent and alternative bases

Consent is only one possible basis for processing and is often inappropriate where participation is mandatory, bargaining power is unequal, or withdrawal cannot realistically be honoured.

ONDTF governance SHALL identify the applicable authority or basis for each processing purpose, the party responsible for that determination, the affected person's choices, and the consequences of refusal or withdrawal.

Consent, when used, SHOULD be specific, informed, unambiguous, recorded, revocable, and no broader than necessary. Withdrawal SHALL stop future processing unless another valid basis applies; it does not automatically erase accountability records that must lawfully be retained.
