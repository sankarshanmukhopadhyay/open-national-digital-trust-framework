# Registry and status-query privacy

Registry resolution and status checking can reveal who is interacting, which credential or authority is being evaluated, when an event occurs, and which organisations have a relationship.

Controls MAY include caching with freshness bounds, privacy-preserving status structures, query aggregation, proxying, oblivious or batch mechanisms, pairwise identifiers, access minimisation, and retention limits.

A profile SHALL document which parties can observe queries, logs, network metadata, and correlation identifiers. Availability and revocation freshness requirements SHALL be balanced against privacy risk explicitly rather than silently.
