# Service lifecycle

Provider recognition and service authorisation are separate. A recognised provider MAY operate only services within an approved service scope and only while each service remains active.

## Service states

A service moves through proposed, assessed, authorised, active, restricted, suspended, retired and archived states. A provider-level event may affect one service, several services or the entire provider, but the decision record must identify the exact affected scope.

## Minimum service record

Each service record MUST include:

- service identifier, provider and accountable operator;
- authorised function and excluded functions;
- jurisdiction and user population;
- assurance claims and dependencies;
- technical endpoints and status mechanisms;
- activation, expiry and review dates;
- restrictions and material conditions;
- incident contact and continuity arrangements;
- current public status and status provenance.

A material service change MUST be classified before deployment and may require reassessment or reauthorisation.
