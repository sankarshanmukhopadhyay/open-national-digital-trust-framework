# Suspension, restriction and withdrawal

Status controls protect relying parties and affected parties when continued unrestricted operation is no longer justified.

## Decision principles

Actions MUST be lawful or governance-authorised, evidence-based, proportionate, time-bounded where appropriate and scoped to the affected provider or service. Emergency action MAY precede a full hearing where delay would create material harm, but prompt notice and independent review MUST follow.

## Status propagation

The decision record MUST identify the effective time, affected services, reason category, permitted residual operations, review date and public disclosure level. Authoritative registries and trust lists MUST propagate status changes within the profile-defined maximum delay.

Withdrawal is final for the affected approval unless a new application is accepted. Suspension is temporary and MUST state reinstatement criteria.
