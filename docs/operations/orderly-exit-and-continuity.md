# Orderly exit and continuity

A provider's exit must not strand participants, conceal status history or destroy evidence needed for challenge, audit or remedy.

An exit plan MUST address notice, cessation dates, transfer or termination of services, credential and status consequences, participant migration, critical records, keys and secrets, subcontractors, outstanding incidents and complaints, legal holds, retention and destruction, and final public status.

The governing or supervisory authority MUST be able to direct continuity measures where abrupt failure would create systemic or rights-related harm.
