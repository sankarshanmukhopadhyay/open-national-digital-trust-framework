# Material change management

A material change is a change capable of altering authority, assurance, risk, interoperability, rights impact or the basis of an existing conformance decision.

## Typical triggers

- ownership, control or governing-body change;
- merger, acquisition or insolvency risk;
- new service, jurisdiction or user population;
- cryptographic, protocol or architecture change;
- critical supplier or hosting change;
- material security, privacy or accessibility incident;
- change to evidence supporting certification;
- change affecting delegated authority or status checking.

The provider MUST notify the designated authority before implementation where practicable. The authority MUST classify the change as administrative, notifiable, reviewable or reassessment-required and record the rationale.
