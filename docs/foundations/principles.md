# Design Principles

1. **Trust is resolved, not declared.** Trust decisions depend on current evidence, policy, authority, context, and risk.
2. **Authority is distinct from identity and capability.** Being identifiable or technically able to act does not establish permission.
3. **Consequential effects require attributable authority.** High-impact actions need evidence of mandate, scope, lineage, and controls.
4. **Governance is executable.** Rules must connect to operational decision points, status, evidence, and enforcement.
5. **Assurance is continuous where risk is continuous.** One-time onboarding cannot substitute for current status and runtime controls.
6. **Disclosure is proportionate.** Systems should reveal only what the decision requires.
7. **Federation is preferred to unnecessary centralisation.** Common semantics and verification can coexist with distributed control.
8. **Redress is part of the architecture.** Challenge, correction, suspension, appeal, and remedy cannot be external afterthoughts.
9. **Profiles preserve pluralism.** Jurisdictions and sectors may specialise the framework while retaining interoperable meaning.
10. **Open standards are selected by capability and risk.** ONDTF does not prescribe technology merely because it is popular.
