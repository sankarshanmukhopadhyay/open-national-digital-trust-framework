# Executive and Policy Path

**Audience:** Sponsors, policymakers, regulators, and programme leaders  
**Prerequisites:** [ONDTF in One Hour](one-hour.md), or equivalent familiarity  
**Estimated time:** 90 minutes  
**Outcome:** a role-specific understanding of the framework, with enough context to use the reference documentation confidently.

## Recommended sequence

| Order | Read | Why it comes here |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | [Framework overview](../index.md) | Establish the policy problem and intended outcome. |
| 2 | [Scope and non-goals](../core-specification/scope-and-non-goals.md) | See what ONDTF does not centralise or prescribe. |
| 3 | [Core principles](../foundations/principles.md) | Understand the non-negotiable design commitments. |
| 4 | [Target operating model](../operating-model/index.md) | See the institutional functions and decision rights. |
| 5 | [Governance framework](../governance/index.md) | Understand authority, delegation, accountability, and redress. |
| 6 | [Security architecture](../security/index.md) | Understand systemic security, not only technical security. |
| 7 | [Worked reference scenario](../reference-scenario/index.md) | Test the framework against a complete case. |
| 8 | [Adoption](../adoption/index.md) | Understand staged adoption and programme choices. |

## Reading method

Read the pages in order on the first pass. On later visits, use the sidebar and search as a reference system. The sequence is curated; it does not imply that later pages are normatively subordinate to earlier ones.
