# Transparency, notice and explanation

Notice SHOULD identify the responsible party, purpose, authority, data used, major decision criteria, disclosures, retention, available choices, challenge route, and expected consequences.

Explanations SHOULD be meaningful for the recipient and proportionate to impact. They need not expose secrets or enable abuse, but SHALL provide enough information to understand the basis of a consequential outcome and contest material errors or misuse of authority.
