# Rights evidence

Rights-related claims require operational evidence. Policy publication alone does not establish that notice, challenge, appeal or remedy works in practice.

Minimum evidence includes channel availability, accessibility testing, acknowledgement and resolution times, independent-review records, remedy completion, propagation checks, repeat-harm analysis and unresolved barriers. Evidence should be disaggregated where lawful and proportionate so structural exclusion is not hidden by aggregate completion rates.

Machine-readable challenge, appeal, remedy and accessibility records support consistent evidence collection while allowing profiles to impose stricter privacy and retention controls.
