# Canonical workflows

The workflow specifications demonstrate how ONDTF capabilities compose. They are technology-neutral and define required evidence, controls and failure behaviour.

- [Trust-scheme establishment](scheme-establishment.md)
- [Participant onboarding](participant-onboarding.md)
- [Authority grant and delegation](authority-delegation.md)
- [Trust resolution and effect admission](trust-resolution.md)
- [Status change and suspension](status-change.md)
- [Policy change and emergency authority](policy-change.md)
- [Incident response](incident-response.md)
- [Challenge, appeal and remedy](challenge-redress.md)
- [Cross-domain recognition](cross-domain-recognition.md)
- [Conformance declaration](conformance-declaration.md)

## Workflow specification rule

Each workflow identifies purpose, actors, preconditions, normal flow, alternatives, failure conditions, evidence produced, and security and privacy obligations. A profile MAY replace the sequence but MUST preserve the required outcomes and evidence.
