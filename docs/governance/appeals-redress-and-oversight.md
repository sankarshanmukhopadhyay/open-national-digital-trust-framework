# Appeals, redress and oversight

Institutional legitimacy requires more than correct initial decisions. ONDTF separates operational complaints, independent appeal, remedy execution and systemic oversight.

## Complaints and appeals body

The body responsible for complaints and appeals must have authority to:

- obtain relevant records;
- correct administrative errors;
- review provider and scheme decisions;
- order or recommend effective remedies;
- escalate matters outside its competence;
- protect complainants from retaliation;
- communicate reasons and timelines accessibly.

Final review must be institutionally independent of the original decision where the consequence is material.

## Independent oversight

Independent Oversight evaluates patterns that individual cases may not reveal, including exclusion, discriminatory effects, surveillance expansion, concentration of authority, recurring provider failures and ineffective remedies.

Oversight findings should feed controlled change, supervisory priorities and public reporting.
