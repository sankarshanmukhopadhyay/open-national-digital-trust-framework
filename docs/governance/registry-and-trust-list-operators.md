# Registry and trust-list operators

A Registry or Trust-list Operator publishes governed status or discovery information. It does not determine the underlying legal authority merely by publishing an entry.

## Responsibilities

- publish the governing authority and scope of the register;
- accept changes only from authorised sources;
- maintain provenance, effective time and status history;
- apply authenticated and authorised administrative changes;
- support timely suspension, revocation and correction;
- protect availability, integrity, confidentiality and query privacy as applicable;
- expose version and freshness information to relying parties;
- preserve evidence sufficient to investigate disputed entries;
- provide recovery and continuity arrangements.

## Reliance boundary

A relying party must evaluate whether a registry entry is relevant and sufficient for the intended decision. Presence in a register is not a universal guarantee of legality, competence or fitness for purpose.
