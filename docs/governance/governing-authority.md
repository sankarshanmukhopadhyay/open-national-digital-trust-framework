# Governing authority

The Governing Authority is accountable for the legitimacy, scope and continuing coherence of an ONDTF adoption. It may be a statutory body, a designated public authority, a multi-stakeholder scheme authority or another institution with a demonstrable mandate.

## Minimum responsibilities

The Governing Authority must:

- publish its mandate, jurisdiction and decision scope;
- approve the framework profile and material changes;
- designate or recognise administrative, supervisory, assessment and review functions;
- define public-interest objectives and prohibited uses;
- approve risk appetite and non-waivable requirements;
- ensure sustainable funding without compromising independent oversight;
- establish transparency, consultation and change-control processes;
- preserve accessible challenge, appeal and remedy;
- periodically review institutional effectiveness and concentration risk.

## Authority boundaries

The Governing Authority should not become a universal transaction intermediary. It must not use framework governance as an implied entitlement to inspect all transactions, centralise unnecessary personal information or override competent legal process.

Emergency powers must be purpose-bound, time-limited, recorded and independently reviewed.

## Evidence

Expected evidence includes the constituting mandate, approved profile, published decision-rights register, meeting and consultation records, risk-acceptance decisions, designation instruments and periodic accountability reports.
