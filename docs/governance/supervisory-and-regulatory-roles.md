# Supervision and regulatory roles

Supervision determines whether admitted providers, services and institutional bodies continue to satisfy applicable obligations. Regulatory authority arises from law; ONDTF does not create it.

## Supervisory responsibilities

A Supervisory Authority should:

- maintain a risk-based supervision plan;
- receive assessment, incident and material-change information;
- investigate suspected nonconformity or harm;
- direct remediation where authorised;
- impose, recommend or record restrictions, suspension and withdrawal;
- coordinate with privacy, competition, consumer, cybersecurity and sector regulators;
- publish proportionate status and enforcement information;
- preserve procedural fairness and appeal rights.

## Regulatory coordination

Where several regulators have overlapping competence, the operating model must define referral, lead-authority and information-sharing rules. A provider must not be forced to resolve institutional ambiguity during an active incident or affected-party complaint.
