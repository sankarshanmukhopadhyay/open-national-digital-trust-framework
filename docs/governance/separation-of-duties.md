# Separation of duties

ONDTF does not require a separate institution for every role. It requires that incompatible powers are recognised and controlled.

## High-risk combinations

| Combination | Principal risk | Minimum safeguard |
|---|---|---|
| Rule setter and service operator | Rules favour the operator or suppress alternatives | Transparent consultation, independent review and published conflicts |
| Operator and assessor | Self-assessment becomes the only assurance basis | Independent assessment for material claims |
| Supervisor and final appeals body | Enforcement decision cannot be fairly challenged | Separate appeal panel or external review |
| Registry operator and status authority | Unauthorised or self-serving status changes | Dual control, provenance and reviewable change records |
| Provider and remedy adjudicator | Harm is minimised or remedy delayed | Independent escalation and enforceable timelines |
| Accreditation authority and commercial assessor | Market access is distorted | Impartiality controls and external oversight |

## Concentration assessment

Where one institution performs several roles, it must document:

- legal basis;
- operational necessity;
- conflict analysis;
- compensating controls;
- independent decision points;
- data-access boundaries;
- audit and transparency measures;
- review and deconcentration triggers.
