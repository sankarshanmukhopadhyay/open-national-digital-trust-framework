# Accreditation and assessment roles

Accreditation and conformity assessment are distinct functions.

- The **Accreditation Authority** determines whether an assessment body is competent, impartial and adequately governed for an assessment scope.
- A **Conformity Assessment Body** evaluates a provider, service, implementation or organisation against identified requirements.
- An **Assessor** performs defined evaluation activities under the authority and quality system of an assessment body or recognised scheme.

## Minimum safeguards

- assessment scope and competence must be explicit;
- assessor independence and conflicts must be evaluated;
- assessment evidence and sampling must be reproducible enough for review;
- material nonconformities must be recorded rather than negotiated away;
- certification or recognition decisions must be separated from commercial delivery pressure;
- complaints about an assessor must have an escalation route beyond that assessor;
- surveillance and reassessment must be proportionate to change and risk.

Detailed certification lifecycle requirements are developed in the v0.6.0 conformance commit. This foundation establishes the institutional boundary and accountability model.
