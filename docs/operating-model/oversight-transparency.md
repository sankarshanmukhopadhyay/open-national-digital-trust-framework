# Oversight and transparency

Oversight must be capable of examining not only technical compliance but also authority, institutional performance, rights impacts, market concentration, and the effectiveness of remedies.

## Minimum transparency set

- current governance bodies and decision rights;
- applicable profiles, policies, and effective dates;
- recognised trust schemes, participants, assessors, and authoritative sources;
- material changes and decision rationales;
- service availability and status performance;
- aggregate incidents and response outcomes;
- complaints, appeal durations, and remedies;
- assurance limitations and unresolved risks;
- conflicts of interest and recusals;
- scheduled independent reviews.

Publication must preserve legitimate confidentiality, security, and personal-data protections. Those protections should not be used to conceal institutional accountability.
