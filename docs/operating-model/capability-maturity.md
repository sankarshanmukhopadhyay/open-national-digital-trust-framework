# Capability maturity

The maturity model supports staged adoption without allowing early stages to be misrepresented as full conformance.

| Level | Characterisation | Typical evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 0 — Fragmented | Trust functions exist independently with unclear accountability | Isolated policies and services |
| 1 — Defined | Scope, mandate, roles, and priority schemes are documented | Charter, profile, authority map |
| 2 — Governed | Lifecycle, change, assurance, incident, and redress processes operate | Process records and service measures |
| 3 — Federated | Sector and regional schemes interoperate under common recognition rules | Federation agreements and tests |
| 4 — Measured | Performance, risk, assurance, and remedy effectiveness are continuously evaluated | Dashboards, assessments, trend evidence |
| 5 — Adaptive | Policy and controls evolve from evidence while preserving rights and accountability | Controlled change and independent review |

A jurisdiction profile should declare its maturity by capability rather than assigning one undifferentiated national score.
