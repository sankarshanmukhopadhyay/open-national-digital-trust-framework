# Exceptions, waivers and risk acceptance

An exception records a temporary or bounded departure from a requirement. A waiver records an authorised decision not to apply a requirement within a declared scope. Neither removes accountability or converts non-conformance into conformance.

Every record MUST include:

- requirement and affected boundary;
- business or public-interest rationale;
- risk analysis and affected parties;
- compensating controls;
- accountable approver and authority basis;
- start, expiry and review dates;
- monitoring conditions;
- remediation or exit plan;
- disclosure classification.

Exceptions MUST expire automatically unless renewed through a fresh decision. Material exceptions SHOULD be visible to assessors and relying parties whose decisions depend on the affected capability.
