# Assurance evaluation and conclusions

An assurance evaluation compares evidence against declared criteria and produces a conclusion that is limited by scope, time, independence and uncertainty.

## Required conclusion fields

1. subject and assessed boundary;
2. applicable ONDTF version and profile;
3. claims and criteria evaluated;
4. evidence cut-off date;
5. evaluator identity, competence and independence disclosure;
6. findings, exceptions and unresolved risks;
7. conclusion and permitted reliance;
8. expiry or reassessment trigger.

## Conclusion states

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Satisfied | criteria are met within the assessed scope |
| Satisfied with conditions | use is permitted subject to stated controls or time limits |
| Partially satisfied | material criteria are incomplete and reliance must be constrained |
| Not satisfied | criteria are not met |
| Indeterminate | available evidence cannot support a reliable conclusion |

A conclusion MUST NOT collapse multiple assurance dimensions into one unexplained score. Identity assurance, authority assurance, service assurance, control assurance, governance assurance and operational assurance remain distinct.
