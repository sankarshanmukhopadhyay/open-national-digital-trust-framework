# Identity assurance

Identity assurance evaluates whether the subject relevant to a decision has been established with evidence appropriate to the risk and context. It covers enrolment, binding, continuity, recovery, duplicate or synthetic identity risks, and the provenance and freshness of identity evidence.

A profile must state the subject type, evidence sources, proofing method, binding mechanism, recovery process, permitted pseudonymity and any population-specific limitations. Identity assurance does not establish authority, entitlement, delegation or legitimate exercise.
