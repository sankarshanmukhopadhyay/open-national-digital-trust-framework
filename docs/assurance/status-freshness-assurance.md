# Status and freshness assurance

Status and freshness assurance evaluates whether lifecycle-dependent facts are current enough for the decision risk. It covers revocation, suspension, expiry, trust-list status, accreditation, delegation and incident restrictions.

Profiles must define authoritative status sources, maximum acceptable age, caching rules, failure semantics, offline operation and propagation objectives. When required freshness cannot be established, the outcome must be explicit: refuse, degrade, defer, seek alternative evidence or invoke a governed exception.
