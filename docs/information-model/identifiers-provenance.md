# Identifiers and Provenance

Every governed object must have a stable identifier within its authority domain, a declared issuing or controlling authority, a version or revision indicator, and provenance sufficient to establish where it originated and how it changed.

## Minimum provenance fields

- identifier;
- object type;
- controlling authority;
- issued or created time;
- effective time;
- expiry time where applicable;
- version or revision;
- predecessor or lineage reference;
- integrity mechanism;
- publication or resolution location;
- lifecycle status;
- applicable jurisdiction and profile.

Identifiers must not imply assurance by their syntax alone. Assurance derives from authority, evidence, controls, status, and evaluation context.
