# Scope and non-goals

## Scope

ONDTF provides a baseline for the governance and operation of digital trust infrastructure at national or comparable jurisdictional scale. It addresses interactions in which identity, authority, evidence, policy, assurance, accountability, and redress materially affect a person, organisation, public body, or automated system.

The core covers:

- institutional and delegated authority;
- trust-scheme governance;
- participant admission, suspension, and exit;
- identity, role, and authority resolution;
- policy and obligation evaluation;
- evidence and credential lifecycle;
- registries, status services, and authoritative sources;
- assurance and control evaluation;
- consequential trust decisions and admitted effects;
- security, privacy, incident handling, challenge, and redress;
- sector, jurisdiction, and technical profiling;
- interoperability and conformance.

## Non-goals

ONDTF does not:

- prescribe a national identity system;
- require centralisation of registries or decision services;
- mandate a particular cryptographic mechanism, credential format, protocol, or ledger;
- replace legislation, regulation, judicial process, or sector supervision;
- certify products or organisations by itself;
- assume that technical verification establishes lawful authority, legitimacy, or acceptable consequences;
- treat trust as a single score;
- require adoption of TSMM, TIS, or any other external project.

## Applicability rule

An adopting authority SHOULD document the classes of interaction to which its ONDTF profile applies, the effects that are considered consequential, and the institutions empowered to make or review the resulting decisions.
