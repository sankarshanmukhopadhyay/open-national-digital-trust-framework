# Actor and Role Model

The framework recognises the following actor classes:

- natural persons;
- legal persons and organisations;
- public authorities;
- regulated professionals;
- devices and workloads;
- digital services and platforms;
- autonomous and semi-autonomous software agents;
- guardians, delegates, fiduciaries and representatives;
- auditors, assessors and supervisory bodies.

An actor may hold several roles in different transactions. Roles include governance authority, issuer, holder, subject, verifier, relying party, registry operator, wallet provider, assurance provider, policy decision point, evidence custodian and redress authority.

Role separation SHOULD be used to reduce conflicts of interest. A credential issuer SHOULD NOT be the sole authority determining whether its own credential is sufficient for all relying-party purposes.
