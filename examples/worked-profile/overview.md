# Essential Services Access DTF

## Purpose

The profile enables controlled use of verifiable evidence for access to essential public and regulated services. It is designed around a harder requirement than successful verification: an affected person must be able to understand, challenge and obtain correction or remedy when evidence, authority, policy execution or status information is wrong.

## Scope

Included use cases are benefit eligibility confirmation, regulated utility onboarding and assisted service access. The profile supports authorised representatives but excludes law-enforcement identification, population surveillance and automated final eligibility determination.

## Governing outcomes

- evidence is used only under demonstrable authority;
- providers and services remain subject to lifecycle controls and surveillance;
- assurance is multidimensional and critical weaknesses cannot be averaged away;
- adverse outcomes remain explainable and contestable;
- external recognition is conditional, evidenced and reversible;
- technology dependencies remain subordinate to governance requirements.

## Profile status

The worked profile is **illustrative and validation-complete**, while its legal authority remains explicitly marked for adopter replacement. This distinction demonstrates that technical completeness does not manufacture institutional legitimacy.
