# Risk, recognition and maintenance

## Priority risk controls

| Risk | Preventive control | Detection | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stale status | bounded freshness policy | status-age telemetry | fail safely or step up review |
| Issuer compromise | trust-list and key controls | anomaly and incident signals | suspend affected evidence paths |
| Provider collusion | separation of duties | independent audit | restriction, investigation and remedy |
| Wrongful correlation | purpose and minimisation controls | privacy review | delete linkage and notify affected parties |
| Delegation misuse | scoped and time-bounded authority | delegation event audit | revoke and reconsider affected outcomes |
| Registry outage | resilience and cached-status rules | availability monitoring | degraded-mode controls and notice |
| Remedy obstruction | service-level and evidence duties | complaint ageing | escalation to independent oversight |

## Recognition

External frameworks are recognised only through documented conditional equivalence. Recognition is profile-specific and can be suspended immediately when authority, assurance, status or remedy compatibility materially fails.

## Maintenance

Controlled documents are reviewed annually and after legal change, material incidents, dependency changes or assurance failures. Breaking changes normally receive 180 days' notice. Emergency changes require retrospective review within 30 days. Retirement requires a migration plan, rights-impact review and public notice.
