# ONDTF v0.6.0 — Operational Framework Draft

**Release date:** 24 July 2026

## Release summary

v0.6.0 transforms ONDTF from a feature-complete architectural baseline into an operational framework draft. It now describes who may decide, under what authority, through which lifecycle, against what evidence, with which assurance and conformance controls, and how affected parties can obtain notice, challenge, independent review and remedy.

The release also introduces Guided Framework Construction as a governed adaptation mechanism. Adopters can work through structured decisions, reusable patterns, contradiction rules and completeness gates to produce a DTF package while preserving provenance, unresolved matters and required review gates.

## Principal capabilities

- normative obligations assigned to defined institutional roles;
- provider and service admission, activation, monitoring, change, suspension, withdrawal, reinstatement and exit;
- conformance claims, assessor competence, accreditation, certification lifecycle and appeal;
- multidimensional assurance with critical non-compensable floors;
- affected-party notice, explanation, correction, appeal, accessibility and remedy;
- governed jurisdiction, sector, technology, recognition and operational profiles;
- controlled-document, standards-reference and profile maintenance;
- formal external-framework pattern adoption;
- machine-readable Guided Framework Construction;
- a validated fictional Essential Services Access DTF worked profile.

## Issue resolution

The release closes URI-07, URI-08, URI-09, URI-10, URI-11, URI-12, URI-18 and URI-19. Accessibility and remedy architecture is complete, while URI-06 and URI-17 remain open for real pilot evidence. Independent implementation, executable conformance, empirical calibration, adversarial exercises, recognition and interoperability evidence remain scheduled for later releases.

## Release boundary

Operational Framework Draft means that the operating architecture is complete enough for structured review, profiling and implementation planning. It does not mean that ONDTF is production-certified, legally approved for a jurisdiction, empirically calibrated for every sector or independently demonstrated across multiple implementations.

## Upgrade guidance

Adopters of v0.5.0 should review the normative catalogue, role assignments, provider lifecycle, conformance model, assurance dimensions, rights and remedy model, profile architecture and maintenance controls. Existing profiles should be re-expressed using the profile manifest and guided-construction decision semantics where practical.
